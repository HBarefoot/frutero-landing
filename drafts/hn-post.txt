Show HN: Open-source mushroom grow controller for Raspberry Pi

I built Frutero because I was tired of checking 4 humidifiers every morning before work.

It's a local-first controller that runs on a Pi 4B: schedules lights, mists by humidity threshold, streams a USB camera, and has an AI advisor (Claude/Ollama) that reads sensor trends + camera images to flag contamination and pinning issues.

Key point: it's fully functional on the Pi without signing up for anything. No account. No cloud dependency. Your data stays on your SD card.

The cloud Fleet tier is optional and only for people running >1 chamber who want one dashboard and remote monitoring.

Hardware: Pi 4B, 2-channel relay, DHT22, USB camera, ultrasonic mister (~$40 in parts). Full GPIO pinout in the repo.

GitHub: https://github.com/HBarefoot/frutero
Landing + free trial: https://frutero-landing.vercel.app

What I learned building it: GPIO scheduling that survives reboots, boot-time state restoration, and why you need safety clamps when a mister can destroy a batch by dry-firing.
